Recommended Focus Areas — March 2026
Four areas with the highest potential impact for the coming month. Directly derived from what the data shows and what staff have said in their own words.
Priority 1 · Urgent
Post-Shift Recovery — Back of House
Post-shift Physical scores drop to 2.03. BOH Recovery averages 2.81. Kitchen staff describe exhaustion, chronic full-body pain, and being unable to decompress after service. The single most urgent area in the data.
Suggested action: Introduce a short structured wind-down at the end of kitchen shifts — 5–10 minutes of guided stretching or breathing at a fixed time. Low cost, directly responsive to what staff have explicitly said.
Priority 2 · Structural
Shift Predictability & Working Environment Structure
Survey responses consistently name working hours, unpredictability, and lack of planning as the core professional stressor. 80% of all low scores are work-attributed — management intervention can make a real difference here.
Suggested action: Review shift communication practices — how much notice staff have, clarity of expectations at start of service, and consistency of break allocation.
Priority 3 · Investment
Skill Development — Honour the Top Priority
"Skills to do the job better" is the highest-rated priority in both surveys (2.83–2.85 out of 3). Staff are not asking for sympathy — they are asking to be developed. A meaningful distinction for how this programme is framed.
Suggested action: One targeted learning moment per month — product tasting, technique session, guest speaker. Even a single structured learning event signals real investment in staff capability.
Priority 4 · Programme Health
Post-Shift Check-in Participation
167 check-ins is a strong start. Only 34 (20%) were post-shift — where the most important signals appear. The post-shift data reveals the true cost of each service and needs consistent capture.
Suggested action: A brief reminder at shift handover that the post-shift Pulse takes 60 seconds and is the most valuable check-in of the day. Emphasise anonymity. Frame it as staff having their say.
